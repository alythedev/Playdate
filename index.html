What is this?

Playdate is a YSWS where you (and a friend) need to make a game intended to be played by 2 people. The theme and type of game is totally up to you.

The easiest approach to this YSWS would be a game where both players are playing on the same device, like in 'Fireboy & Watergirl'.

How do I participate?

1. Find a "playdate"

Find a friend to make a game with you! If they're not already in the Hack Club Slack, you can invite them yourself.

2. Post your idea in #playdate

Introduce your game idea, who you're working with, and what you each plan to do.

3. Code/draw

You and your "playdate" should track your time seperately. You should both be using Hackatime (for time spent coding) and Lapse (for time spent on art). Make sure both are set up before you start. You should also have a GitHub repository with frequent commits.

4. Share progress

Post detailed updates to your original thread in #playdate every 5 hours of work.

5. Publish & get prizes!

Publish your game on Itch.io, and submit the form to get your prizes!

Did someone say prizes?

*There are 5 tiers of prizes, depending on the combined hours spent by you and your "playdate".
At each subsequent tier, you get the prizes of all previous tiers. Prizes are intended to be shared.

FAQ

If you still have questions, ask in #playdate-help!

Who is eligible?

Playdate is for all teens aged 13-18 (inclusive). To participate, you'll have to join the Hack Club Slack.

How do I track my time?

You and your "playdate" should be tracking your time seperately. Use Hackatime for time spent coding and Lapse for time spent on art.

What game engines am I allowed to use?

We recommend Godot, but any engine with Hackatime support support should be fine.

What's required for me to submit?

You'll need your thread, total time logged on Hackatime/Lapse, your game deployed on Itch.io, and a public GitHub repository.

Is this double-dippable?

No, you can't submit your Playdate hours to other YSWS programs.

When does this end?

July 30th 2026!
